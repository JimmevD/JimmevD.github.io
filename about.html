About Me

Hi, I am Jimme van Duijnhoven, a Game Design student at Saxion University of Applied Sciences.

Ever since I was a kid, I have loved playing video games like Mario Kart and LEGO games. While playing them, I often thought to myself, "I want to make games like these too," because I wanted other people to experience the same fun and excitement that I did.

I started by studying Game Development, where I learned how to program games. I was incredibly excited that I finally got to create my own games. Eventually, I realized that while I enjoyed programming, it didn't push my creativity enough. That's when I switched to Game Design, and I quickly discovered that I had made the right choice.

I became especially interested in Level Design because it plays such an important role in shaping the player's experience. I enjoy creating environments that are both engaging and memorable, and I love the challenge of building levels that guide and surprise players in meaningful ways. I also love playtesting because it's one of the best ways to improve a game. It's fascinating to see the different solutions players come up with and how each playtest provides valuable feedback that helps improve the design.

My technical background allows me to quickly prototype and implement my own ideas, making it easier to experiment, iterate, and communicate with programmers, artists, and fellow designers. Although level design is where my passion lies, I enjoy being a versatile designer who can contribute throughout development. Whether I'm designing gameplay systems, building prototypes, or creating design documents, I'm always looking for ways to improve the player experience.

Besides designing games, I love swimming, watching shows, taking walks, meeting friends, and, of course, playing games, who knows, I might even find some inspiration along the way ;).
